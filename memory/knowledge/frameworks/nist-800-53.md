# NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5

**Scope**: Security and privacy controls for federal information systems and organizations. The foundational baseline that FISMA + FedRAMP + many state programs derive from.

**Authority**: NIST.

**Total controls**: ~325 in the Moderate baseline (full catalog ~1,000+ across all baselines + privacy + supplemental). 20 control families.

## Control families

AC (Access Control), AT (Awareness/Training), AU (Audit/Accountability), CA (Assessment/Authorization/Monitoring), CM (Configuration Management), CP (Contingency Planning), IA (Identification/Authentication), IR (Incident Response), MA (Maintenance), MP (Media Protection), PE (Physical/Environment), PL (Planning), PM (Program Management), PS (Personnel Security), PT (PII Processing/Transparency), RA (Risk Assessment), SA (System/Services Acquisition), SC (System/Communications Protection), SI (System/Information Integrity), SR (Supply Chain Risk Management).

## Sample mappings

Mirrors [[fedramp]] (FedRAMP Moderate is a parametrized subset). All Edge telemetry events listed in [[edge-telemetry-catalog]] map to controls under AC, AU, CM, IA, IR, SC, SI families.

## Management assertion template

```
Technijian's Authorizing Official affirms that for system [SYSTEM_NAME] at
the [LOW|MOD|HIGH] impact level, controls per NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 are
implemented and operating effectively. POA&Ms tracked: [N]. Continuous
monitoring reports submitted per the CM Strategy.

Signed: [Authorizing Official]
Date: [DATE]
```

## Signing officer

Authorizing Official (federal AO for federal systems; CEO for private adoption).

## Audit cycle

Per Authorization to Operate (typically 3 years) + annual reassessment + continuous monitoring.

## Retention

Per federal records retention. Continuous monitoring artifacts: retained per organizational CM Strategy.
